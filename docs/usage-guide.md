## Opening the App for the First Time

1. Open your phone's web browser (Chrome, Safari, or Firefox)
2. Go to **cgt.ajithrn.com**
3. The app will load — it looks and feels like a regular phone app
4. After a few seconds, you may see a message asking if you'd like to install the app. We recommend saying **Yes** (see next section)

> **Note:** The very first time, you need internet to load the app. After that, it works without internet.

---

## Installing on Your Phone

When you see the "Install Census GeoTagger" message at the bottom of the screen:

1. Tap the **Install** button
2. The app will be added to your home screen
3. You can now open it just like any other app — no need to open the browser each time

**Why install?**
- Opens faster (no browser address bar)
- Works fully offline
- Feels like a native app
- Won't accidentally close when you switch apps

If you missed the install message, you can also:
- **On Android Chrome:** Tap the three-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen"
- **On iPhone Safari:** Tap the share button → "Add to Home Screen"

## Setting Up Your Profile

Before starting surveys, set up your profile so the app automatically fills in your details for every new visit.

1. Tap the **More** button (three dots icon at the bottom-right)
2. Tap **Profile**
3. Fill in:
   - **Surveyor Name** — your full name
   - **Default Ward / Area** — the ward or area you're surveying (e.g., "Ward 5" or "Sector B")
   - **Default Language** — the primary language spoken in your area (e.g., "Malayalam")
   - **Organization** — your department or organization name
4. Tap **Save Profile**

These values will be automatically filled in when you start a new survey, saving you time. You can still change them for individual visits if needed.

## Conducting a Household Survey

The survey form has 6 steps. You move through them one at a time using the **Next** button.

### Step 1: Location (GPS & Address)

This is the most important step — it records where the house is located.

1. Tap the **"Capture GPS Location"** button
2. Your phone will ask for permission to use your location — tap **Allow**
3. Wait a moment until you see the latitude, longitude, and accuracy numbers appear
4. The accuracy number tells you how precise the reading is:
   - Under 10m = Excellent
   - 10–30m = Good
   - Over 30m = Try moving to an open area and tap again
5. Fill in the **Address** (door number, street name)
6. Fill in a **Landmark** (something nearby that helps identify the house, like "near the temple" or "opposite the school")
7. Fill in the **Ward** (this is pre-filled from your profile)
8. Tap **Next**

> **Important:** You must capture GPS before you can proceed. Stand near the house's entrance for the best reading.

### Step 2: Household (Head & Family Members)

1. Enter the **head of household's full name** (the person who answered the door or the primary decision-maker)
2. Enter their **phone number** (optional but helpful for follow-ups)
3. The **primary language** is pre-filled from your profile
4. Count the family members using the **+** and **−** buttons:
   - **Males** — adult males (18 and above)
   - **Females** — adult females (18 and above)
   - **Children** — anyone under 18
   - **Seniors** — anyone 60 years or older
5. The **Total Members** is calculated automatically from Males + Females
6. Tap **Next**

### Step 3: Housing (Type & Facilities)

1. **Ownership** — tap the card that matches:
   - Owned (family owns the house)
   - Rented (paying rent)
   - Government (government quarters)
   - Other
2. **Structure** — what is the house made of:
   - Pucca = concrete, brick, permanent construction
   - Semi-Pucca = mix of permanent and temporary materials
   - Kutcha = mud, thatch, temporary materials
3. **Water Source** — where does the household get water:
   - Tap/Piped water
   - Well
   - Borewell
   - River/Pond
   - Other
4. **Facilities** — toggle these on or off:
   - Has Electricity (green = yes, gray = no)
   - Has Toilet (green = yes, gray = no)
5. Tap **Next**

### Step 4: Economy (Income & Work)

1. **Occupation** — what does the head of household do for work:
   - Agriculture (farming)
   - Business (shop owner, trader)
   - Service (salaried job, government employee)
   - Daily Labour (construction worker, etc.)
   - Other
2. **Monthly Income** — approximate household income per month (select the range)
3. **Ration Card** — what type of ration card does the family have:
   - BPL (Below Poverty Line)
   - APL (Above Poverty Line)
   - AAY (Antyodaya — for the poorest)
   - None
4. Tap **Next**

### Step 5: Health & Education

1. **School-going Children** — how many children in the family currently attend school
2. **Nearest School** — how far is the nearest school in kilometers
3. **Disabled Members** — toggle on if any family member has a disability
4. **Chronic Illness** — toggle on if any family member has a long-term illness (diabetes, heart disease, etc.)
5. Tap **Next**

### Step 6: Review & Save

1. **Surveyor Name** — pre-filled from your profile, change if someone else is recording
2. **Visit Status** — select one:
   - **Completed** — you collected all information successfully
   - **Partial** — some information is missing (they were busy, you'll add later)
   - **Revisit Needed** — you need to come back (no one was home, etc.)
3. **Notes** — write any observations (optional). Examples:
   - "House under construction"
   - "Elderly person living alone"
   - "Asked to come back after 5 PM"
4. Review the **summary** at the bottom — it shows all your collected data
5. Tap **Save Visit**

After saving, you'll see a success message and be taken to the Records page.

## Viewing Your Visits on the Map

1. Tap the **Map** tab at the bottom
2. You'll see all your recorded visits as numbered pins on the map
3. The number on each pin matches the number in your Records list
4. Pin colors indicate the visit status:
   - 🟢 Green = Completed
   - 🟡 Amber = Partial
   - 🔴 Red = Revisit Needed
5. Tap any pin to see a popup with household details
6. Use the filter buttons at the top to show only certain statuses
7. Pinch to zoom in/out, drag to move around
8. Tap the **fullscreen button** (top-right corner) for a larger view

---

## Viewing and Managing Records

1. Tap the **Records** tab at the bottom
2. You'll see a list of all visits in the order you recorded them
3. Each record shows:
   - Numbered pin (matches the map)
   - Head of household name
   - Address and ward
   - Household ID (auto-generated)
   - Date and time of visit
4. **Search**: Use the search box to find records by name, address, ward, or ID
5. **View details**: Tap any record to expand it and see all collected data
6. **Delete**: Tap the Delete button inside an expanded record to remove it

---

## Editing a Previously Saved Visit

If you made a mistake or need to update information:

1. Go to the **Records** tab
2. Tap the record you want to edit to expand it
3. Tap the **Edit** button
4. You'll be taken to the survey form with all the data pre-filled
5. Navigate through the steps and change what you need
6. On the last step, tap **Update Visit**
7. The record will be updated (not duplicated)

## Exporting Your Data

Tap the **Export** tab to see your options:

### CSV (Spreadsheet)
- Opens in Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, LibreOffice
- One row per household, columns for all fields
- Good for: data analysis, sharing with office, uploading to databases

### GeoJSON (Map Data)
- Opens in QGIS, Google Earth, MapBox
- Standard geographic data format with coordinates
- Good for: GIS analysis, creating official maps, spatial reporting

### PDF Report
- A complete printable report with:
  - Map showing all numbered pins
  - Index table (pin number → name, address)
  - Statistics dashboard
  - One detail page per household
- Good for: submitting to supervisors, printing, presentations

### Map Image (PNG)
- High-resolution image of the map with all numbered pins
- Good for: presentations, embedding in Word documents, WhatsApp sharing

## Taking Backups

Your data is stored only on your phone. If you lose your phone or clear the browser data, the data is gone. **Take regular backups.**

1. Tap **More** (three dots) → **Settings**
2. Tap **Export Backup**
3. A file will download to your phone (named like `census-backup-1234567.json`)
4. Move this file to:
   - Google Drive
   - WhatsApp (send to yourself)
   - Email (send to yourself)
   - Any cloud storage

**When to back up:**
- At the end of every field day
- Before updating the app
- Before clearing your browser data
- Whenever you have 5+ new records since the last backup

---

## Restoring from a Backup

If you lost your data or moved to a new phone:

1. Tap **More** → **Settings**
2. Choose one of two options:

### Import & Merge
- Adds records from the backup to your existing data
- If a record already exists (same ID), it's skipped
- Use this when combining data from multiple sessions or devices

### Import & Replace
- **Deletes all current data first**, then restores from the backup
- Use this when recovering from data loss or setting up a new phone
- You'll be asked to confirm before it proceeds

## Working Offline

The app is designed to work without internet:

- **After first visit**: The app is saved to your phone's storage
- **GPS works offline**: Your phone's GPS sensor doesn't need internet
- **Map tiles**: Areas you've already viewed are saved. New areas need internet to load the map background
- **All features work offline**: Recording visits, viewing records, exporting files
- **When you go online**: The app will check for updates and show a notification if a new version is available

> **Tip:** Before going to a new area without internet, open the Map tab and browse the area while you still have internet. This caches the map tiles for offline use.

## Understanding the PDF Report

The PDF report contains multiple pages:

1. **Cover Page**: Shows the map with all numbered pins and a color legend
2. **Map Index**: A table connecting each pin number to the household name, address, and status
3. **Statistics Dashboard**: Summary numbers — total visits, population, percentages for electricity, toilet, occupations, etc.
4. **Household Detail Pages**: One page per household with all collected information, organized by section

The pin numbers are consistent everywhere — the same house is always the same number in the map, the records list, and the PDF.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### The GPS is not accurate. What should I do?
- Move to an open area (away from tall buildings or trees)
- Wait 10–15 seconds after tapping "Capture GPS"
- If accuracy is over 30m, tap the button again to recapture
- GPS works best outdoors with a clear view of the sky

### Can someone else see my data?
- No. All data stays on your phone only
- Nothing is sent to any server or cloud
- The only way data leaves your phone is when YOU export it

### What happens if I clear my browser data?
- All survey data will be lost
- Always take a backup before clearing browser data
- If you installed the app to home screen, clearing browser data may still affect it

### Can two surveyors use the same phone?
- Yes. Just change the surveyor name in the Profile or on each visit
- All records will be in the same database
- You can filter/search by surveyor name in the Records tab

### How many records can the app store?
- There's no fixed limit. IndexedDB (the storage system) can typically hold hundreds of megabytes
- Practically, you can store thousands of records without issues
- If your phone is low on storage, export a backup and clear old data

### The map tiles are not loading
- You need internet to load map tiles for areas you haven't visited before
- Previously viewed areas are cached and work offline
- Try connecting to WiFi and browsing the map area you'll be visiting

### How do I update the app?
- When a new version is available, you'll see a notification at the top
- Tap **Update** to get the latest version
- Your data is never affected by updates
